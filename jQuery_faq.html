Frequently Asked Questions about the National Park Service:

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Jonathan B. Jarvis
What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The Department of the Interior
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Approximately 22,000
How old is the National Park Service?
The National Park Service was created in 1916
How many areas are in the National Park System?
413 areas covering over 84 million acres
How many people visit national parks?
In 2015, there were 307,247,252 visitors
What is the National Park Service budget?
In 2015, the requested budget was $3.65 billion
What is the largest national park?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK
What is the smallest national park?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA
What is the most visited national park?
The most visited national park in 2015 was Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Facts about national parks:

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is 277 miles in length
  • At its widest point, the Grand Canyon is 18 miles wide
  • The Grand Canyon is about 6000 feet deep
  • The Grand Canyon become a national park in 1919

Olympic National Park

  • Olympic National Park covers 922,651 acres
  • Olympic National Park containes 73 miles of wilderness coast
  • There are over 60 named glaciers in Olympic National Park
  • There are 22 endangered species that live in Olympic National Park

Yosemite National Park

  • Yosemite Falls is the tallest waterfall in North America at a height of 2,425 feet
  • Yosemite National Park was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984
  • About 3.5 million people visit Yosemite National Park each year
  • There are 500 giant sequioas in Yosemite National Park
